Every day the use of wind power generated by the energy of 79,000 kilowatts of electricity, electricity enough to supply the park lighting about four hours and twenty minutes or every day at 6 pm to 10 pm.

Star Tunnel

 

It is said that the star tunnel was originally intended for building armor blocks, not for sightseeing. Because of cycling to catch on in recent years, Kaohsiung City Government changed the tunnel into a part of &quot;Cijin Oceanview Bike Path&quot; in 2005, connecting the Cihou Lighthouse, the Cihou Fort and other attractions. It's Taiwan's first oceanview bike path.

 

Shell Museum

Cijin Shell Museum was established in september 2000,renovation in october 2011, the museum collection of more than 2,600 species of shellfish, is the largest museum of shells collection in Taiwan.

Collections are provided by Huang Geliang, a shell collector living in Kaohsiung City. In the collections,which is known as the living fossil of the Nautilus and Rumphius's Slit Shell, with more than 200 kinds of crab specimens show.

Cijin Seaside Park

 

The Cijin Seaside Park is a tourist and recreational facility built by the Kaohsiung Municipal Government in 1993. The Seaside park covers an area of about 45 hectares. It is designed to watch the marine hydrological landscape and set up trails throughout the park, including the beach, natural ecological area, Off-road area, view of the sea landscape trail area, landscape trails about one kilometer, both sides of the bike trail and Cijin beach, the park also has a view of the sea platform, supported by fifteen pillars along the sea.

Sea Pearl

 

Sea-Pearl is located in Kaohsiung Cijin Seashore Park, near the parking lot of the shell museum on the south side,The sea pearl is 10 meters high and 16 meters wide and 9 meters deep. It is the new landmark of Cijin Park and the largest shell art in Asia. The &quot;sea pearl&quot; bathed in the sea breeze and warm sun, with elegant sea shell shape and wonderful golden sky, in the sunset scenery, and face distant ships each other.

Another artwork &quot;listen&quot; is banded around the configuration, used to guide visitors close to the &quot;Sea Pearl&quot;, scattered like shells to carry out, design contains &quot;listening&quot; meaning, Visitors will be able to carefully appreciate the wind and the sea sound, but also hear the deepest heart of the sound.
