Museum of Fine Arts

 

The Kaohsiung Museum of Fine Arts  is located in Gushan District, Kaohsiung, Taiwan. It was founded by the Kaohsiung City Government and has been administrated by the Kaohsiung Bureau of Cultural Affairs since 2003. It occupies about 8.15 acres  and started in 1994. It is the third public arts museum in Taiwan, after the National Taiwan Museum of Fine Arts and the Taipei Fine Arts Museum.

 

The museum is a part of the Neiweipi Cultural Park, which occupies about 40 hectares. The first stage of Neiweipi Cultural Park’s construction started in 1989 and was completed in 1994. During this phase, the Kaohsiung Museum of Fine Arts was completed. Later further developments were made to the entrance plaza, sculpture park, and ecology park.

Chengcing Lake

 

Chengcing Lake is an artificial lake located in Niaosong, Kaohsiung, Taiwan. The lake is not far from downtown Kaohsiung and the major suburban district of Fengshan.The lake is a source of the water supply network and a tourist area of the Kaohsiung region.

 

A portion of the lake with its shore is administrated as a gated park. A chateau of Chiang Kai-shek, the former president and military leader of Taiwan, is located in the park. Around the lake shore, Chiang also established an underground military headquarters, which has been adapted into a public aquarium, the Cheng Ching Lake Exotic Marine Life Museum.It was originally built as a tunnel in 1961, as protection against a nuclear attack.

Niaosong Wetland

 

Niaosong Wetland Park is a park located in Niaosong District, Kaohsiung City, Taiwan (between Chengcing Lake, Cheng Shiu University of Science and Technology and Kaohsiung Chang Gung Memorial Hospital); it is a park that focuses on natural ecology teaching and is supplemented by sightseeing and recreation. It records 93 species of birds, more than 250 species of insects and more than 340 species of plants. It is a good place for outdoor teaching and nature observation. The park is adopted by the Kaohsiung City Wild Bird Society. The total area of ​​Niaosong Wetland Park is 3 hectares, of which 2 hectares are water area.

Kaohsiung Zhongdu Aihe Wetland Park

 

The Zhongdu Ai River Wetland Park, which connects Kaohsiung's urban ecological green corridor, is located between Shiquan and Jiuru Roads on the south side of the Ai River. It is about 2.5 kilometers long and covers an area of ​​more than 7,000 square meters.

In order to create a "sustainable ecological city with waterfront flowers" that integrates with the lives of citizens, Aihe Wetland Park uses ecological engineering methods to construct slopes to recreate the rich ecological habitat of early wetlands. The park has a log building as a tourist interpretation center, as well as wooden pavilions, suspension bridges, tidal water-friendly platforms, and lamps and chairs designed with driftwood as the main axis. The simple and unpretentious atmosphere is also reflected in the natural multi-layered forest planting. Rich forests have reappeared in the urban area. Native Plant Garden

 

The Native Botanical Garden is the only native plant conservation park in Taiwan. It is located on Chongguang Road in Zuoying District, Kaohsiung City, east of Xinzhuangzi Road in Aozaidie and west of Zengzi Road. It was established in 1994 and covers an area of ​​4.66 hectares. The first green park themed around Taiwan's native plants. Currently, there are more than 60 species of native Taiwanese plants from 45 families in the park. The entire park is like a living Taiwanese botanical museum. In addition to having local characteristics, it also has educational and recreational functions.The plantings in the garden are designed in a landscape style, cleverly combining ecological and recreational functions.

 

Each plant in the park has an explanation sign explaining the name, characteristics and special uses of the tree species, which improves the level of public awareness and the quality of greening. The project adopts ecological construction methods and uses natural stone and wood to reduce damage. The park is equipped with entrance square, education and interpretation center, landscape improvement area, teaching and folk plant area, ecological observation trail, plant literature trail, etc.

Ximen Heritage Park

 

Located in Zuoying, the "Fengshan County Old City Ruins Historical Park" is also known as the "Ximen Ruins Park". It preserves the city foundation and walls built during the Qing Dynasty, the air-raid shelters set up during the Japanese occupation, and the family reunion park built by the Nationalist Army after it came to Taiwan. Village walls, trees, etc. Now that it has been developed into a park, the public can see the ruins from different periods in an undecorated environment and witness the rich cultural heritage of the area.

 

Opposite Haiqing Industrial and Commercial Office and beside Bisheng Road, the ruins of the West Gate city foundation are protected by scaffolding. Today, the city foundation still retains the colorful floor tiles added by the family members, as well as the remains of doors and windows next to the fort. It shows that in order to survive, the families of soldiers at that time had to adapt to local conditions and live a difficult life.
