Banping Mountain

 

Zuoying has good mountains and rivers, and the good mountain is Banping Mountain, which is a peculiar hill with coral reef limestone geology containing a large number of remains of coastal organisms. The entrance is divided into east and west sides. On the west side, there is an obvious "Banping Mountain Hiking Trail" archway. There are trails, pavilions, etc. It has been quite famous since the Qing Dynasty. good place to go. The east side used to be a cement mining field, but it has been shut down for the aftermath of water and soil conservation treatment and maintenance. The name and appearance of Banping Mountain are also due to the excessive mining in the past. Since mining was suspended on July 10, 1987, more than 20,000 woody plants and about 340 different plants have been planted.

Banping Lake

 

The flood detention pond in Banping Mountain is also called &quot;Banping Lake&quot;. Lianchitan and Zhouzai Wetland jointly provide shelter and food for migratory birds, and become an important base of the wetland ecological corridor in the North Kaohsiung metropolitan area. The water level is high in summer and dry in winter. During different water depths, Banping Lake will attract different bird species to inhabit here. With the restoration of the Banping Lake Wetland, more birds, aquatic insects and animals will live there in the future, making the ecosystem of Banping Mountain more diverse and rich.

Zhouzai Wetland Park

 

Zhouzai  is located on the bank of the lotus pond in Zuoying. It is an ecological habitat built with ecological engineering methods and has successfully restored a variety of birds, aquatic animals and plants.

 

Entering the park takes an online reservation commentary system. Walking on the original trail without over-development, you can see the blooming lotus on the pond and aquatic plants such as sagittarius, water chestnut, water chestnut, colorful sandpiper, red-crowned water chicken, teal, heron, etc. It is a beautiful scene of many birds such as snails swimming among the aquatic plants. Aquatic insects, snail shellfish, fish, and amphibians and reptiles can thrive here. more than one species of birds. In the crowded and developed high-rise concrete jungle, the 10-hectare wetland is an oasis in the city, providing buffer and shelter for plants, and also a beautiful and peaceful place for human beings to experience nature and respect all things.

Guishan

 

The Guishan Mountaineering Trail is also known as the Little Guishan Mountaineering Trail. Because of the construction of the road, Guishan Mountain is divided into two, and the local residents call it Big and Little Guishan. Daguishan is still a military controlled area and cannot be entered at will, while Xiaoguishan is open to the public for recreation and has paved wooden plank roads for easy walking. The trail is not long, and the slope is gentle. There are many tree shades, tunnels, and remains of bunkers along the way. It is easy and cool to walk.

 

Guishan has convenient transportation and is only a short distance from the MRT station. It is the "Taoyuan in the City" of Kaohsiung City. Although the height is not high, but because the surroundings are flat and there are no tall buildings above five floors, it has a good view, overlooking famous scenic spots such as Dragon and Tiger Pagoda, Spring and Autumn Pavilion, and Lotus Pond.
