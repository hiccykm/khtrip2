Zuoying Yuandi Temple

 

The Yuandi Temple was previously called the "Arctic Great Temple", commonly known as the "Zuoying Great Temple". It is dedicated to "Xuantian God", also known as "Arctic Emperor". The Yuandi Temple was a ancestral temple built by Zheng Chenggong when he returned to Taiwan in the 20th year of Yongli in the late Ming Dynasty. In the 53rd year of Kangxi's construction, it became the "Arctic Great Temple". After many constructions, it was renamed the "Yuandi Temple".

Zuoying Tianfu Palace

 

Tianfu Temple is a temple with a long history. The former reconstruction took 7 years. In the center of the main hall, the altar general is over 3 meters tall and looks so majestic. Many Chinese couplets on the walls on both sides reflect the prestige of the temple. Standing here, you can see the lotus pond in front: the far right is Chunqiu Pavilion, the Dragon Tiger Tower, and the far left is the traditional Confucius Temple.

Zuoying Tzu Chi Palace

Cijin Tianhou

 

The name of the historic site is Qihou Tianhou Palace, commonly known as Qijin Mazu Temple. It is located in Qijin District of Kaohsiung City and worships the sea god Mazu. During the Qianlong period of the Qing Dynasty (18th century), the Mazu Temple was rebuilt by Zhuang Minjiu. The first large-scale construction of the Tianhou Palace was in the 13th year of Guangxu (1887). Preserve its ancient awkward appearance. The city is currently a historic site.

Fengshan City Grandpa

 

Fengshan Chenghuang Temple was built in 5 years of Jiaqing in Qing Dynasty. Stepping into the temple, you can see two special cultural relics, "you are here" wooden plaque and "big abacus". "You're here" indicates that sooner or later, life will be reported to the grandfather of the city. It is vigorous and powerful; the "big abacus" is a symbol of fine calculation of life's work and good and evil.
